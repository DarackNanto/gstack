## 1. CORPORATE & COMPANY LAW

### Companies Act 2015 (No. 17 of 2015)
The primary statute governing all Kenya-incorporated companies.

**Key coverage:**
- Company formation, types, registration
- Directors: duties, liabilities, disqualification
- Shareholders: rights, meetings, resolutions
- Share capital: classes, issuance, transfers
- Debentures and charges
- Winding up and insolvency
- Annual returns and filing obligations

**Nanto relevance:** Corporate governance, Darack's director duties, annual filing compliance, any future share issuance, Mudharabah structure as a commercial arrangement.

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Companies Act 2015" or "No. 17 of 2015"

- Part XXIV: Winding up

## 2. CONTRACT & COMMERCIAL LAW

### Law of Contract Act (Cap. 23)
The foundational statute for all contracts in Kenya (heavily based on English common law).

**Key coverage:**
- Offer, acceptance, consideration — the essentials of a valid contract
- Capacity to contract
- Void, voidable, and unenforceable contracts
- Misrepresentation and fraud
- Discharge of contract (performance, frustration, breach)
- Remedies: damages, specific performance, injunction

**Nanto relevance:** Every agreement Nanto signs — supplier contracts, NDAs, investor term sheets, distribution agreements.

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Law of Contract Act Cap 23"

**Key principle (well established):** An NDA, non-solicitation clause, or confidentiality agreement is enforceable in Kenya under contract law provided there is offer, acceptance, and consideration (the exchange of confidential information is sufficient consideration).

**Note on restraint of trade:** Non-compete clauses (restricting a party from working in the same industry) are enforceable in Kenya only if they are reasonable in scope, geography, and duration. Courts will strike down overly broad restrictions. Non-solicitation of specific customers or employees is easier to enforce than sector-wide non-competes.

---

### Contracts in Restraint of Trade Act (Cap. 24)
The dedicated Kenya statute governing non-compete and non-solicitation provisions. Works alongside Cap. 23 — Cap. 23 governs contract enforceability generally; Cap. 24 governs the specific validity of restraint-of-trade clauses.

**Key coverage:**
- Section 2: A restraint of trade is void unless the party seeking to enforce it proves it is reasonable having regard to the interests of the parties AND the public interest
- Courts assess three dimensions: (1) nature and scope of the restriction; (2) geographic area; (3) duration
- A restraint clause with no geographic scope or no time period is at risk of being struck down
- Non-solicitation of specific named customers or employees is treated more favourably than sector-wide non-competes
- The burden of proving reasonableness falls on the party seeking to enforce the restraint

**Nanto relevance — CRITICAL for NDAs and employment contracts:**
- Every NDA non-solicitation clause (customers, suppliers, employees) must state: (1) what is restricted; (2) geographic scope (e.g., "within Kenya and the East African Community"); (3) duration (e.g., "during the term of this Agreement and for one (1) year after termination")
- Omitting any of the three dimensions risks the clause being struck down under Section 2
- Applies to: investor NDAs, supplier agreements, employment contracts, distribution agreements
- Live text confirmed: fetched from kenyalaw.org during NDA v2.0 review session (11 May 2026)

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Contracts in Restraint of Trade Act Cap 24"

**Key section:** Section 2 — reasonableness test; burden on the party seeking enforcement

### Sale of Goods Act (Cap. 31)
Governs contracts for the sale of goods — implied terms about title, quality, fitness for purpose.

**Nanto relevance:** All supply agreements for dialysis spare parts, dialyzers, bloodlines. Implied warranty of merchantability and fitness for purpose applies — Nanto must ensure products meet quality standards.

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Sale of Goods Act Cap 31"

---

### Arbitration Act 1995 (Cap. 49)
Governs arbitration proceedings in Kenya. Kenya has adopted the UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration.

**Key coverage:**
- Arbitration agreements are enforceable and courts will stay proceedings where a valid arbitration clause exists
- Recognition and enforcement of foreign arbitral awards
- NCIA (Nairobi Centre for International Arbitration) operates under this Act

**Nanto relevance:** All investor NDAs and agreements should include a Kenya arbitration clause referencing NCIA. This is enforceable.

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Arbitration Act 1995 Cap 49"

---

### Limitation of Actions Act (Cap. 22)
Sets time limits within which legal actions must be brought.

**Key periods (TRAINING — verify):**
- Contract claims: 6 years from breach
- Tort (negligence, fraud): 3 years from discovery
- Land: 12 years

**Nanto relevance:** Customer payment disputes, supplier claim disputes. Ensure contracts include governing law and limitation provisions.

## 3. HEALTHCARE & MEDICAL DEVICE REGULATION

### Pharmacy and Poisons Act (Cap. 244)
The primary statute governing pharmaceutical and medical device regulation in Kenya. The Pharmacy and Poisons Board (PPB) derives its authority from this Act.

**Key coverage:**
- Establishment licensing for importers, distributors, wholesalers of drugs and medical devices
- Product registration: Classes A, B, C (based on risk level)
- Superintendent pharmacist requirements
- Labeling and packaging requirements
- Advertising restrictions

**Nanto relevance — critical:**
- Nanto Med must hold an Establishment License before commercial activity
- Spare parts (Class A devices): product registration per item required
- Dialyzers/bloodlines (Class B/C): require superintendent pharmacist on record
- Application via PRIMS: prims.pharmacyboardkenya.org

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Pharmacy and Poisons Act Cap 244"
**PPB website:** https://www.pharmacyboardkenya.org/

---

### Health Act 2017 (No. 21 of 2017)
Provides the constitutional framework for healthcare in Kenya (right to health, health system structure, regulation of health facilities).

**Key coverage:**
- Licensing of health facilities
- Health products and technologies
- Regulation of health workers
- County vs. national government health functions

**Nanto relevance:** Any future move into clinic operations or health facility management. Currently relevant for understanding the regulatory environment Nanto's customers operate in.

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Health Act 2017"

---

### Social Health Insurance Act 2023
Establishes the Social Health Authority (SHA), replacing the National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF). Governs health insurance contributions, claims processing, and reimbursement rates.

**Key coverage:**
- Mandatory contributions for all employed Kenyans
- Tariff schedule for health services (set by SHA and published in the Gazette)
- Claims submission and reimbursement process
- Accreditation of health facilities for SHA claims

**Nanto relevance — critical:**
- Nanto Med's customers (dialysis centres) depend on SHA reimbursement for revenue
- SHA tariffs for hemodialysis directly determine centres' ability to pay suppliers
- NantoHealth Claims product is built around SHA claims workflow
- SHA tariff updates appear in the Kenya Gazette — monitor weekly

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Social Health Insurance Act 2023"
**SHA website:** https://sha.go.ke/

---

### Kenya Medical Supplies Authority Act 2013 (KEMSA Act)
Governs the Kenya Medical Supplies Authority — the government's medical procurement body.

**Nanto relevance:** KEMSA is a potential large customer for consumables if Nanto wins government tenders. Understanding KEMSA's procurement rules is important for any future tender.

## 4. DATA PROTECTION & DIGITAL

### Data Protection Act 2019 (No. 24 of 2019)
Kenya's primary data privacy law, broadly equivalent to GDPR in structure.

**Key coverage:**
- Personal data: any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
- Data controllers and processors must register with ODPC
- Lawful bases for processing: consent, contract, legitimate interest, legal obligation
- Data subject rights: access, correction, deletion, objection
- Cross-border transfers: only to countries with adequate protection
- Penalties: up to KES 3 million or 2% of annual turnover

**Nanto relevance:**
- Patient/customer data collected through NantoHealth Claims
- WhatsApp communications with facility staff (names, contact details are personal data)
- Supplier and employee personal data
- Must register as a data controller with ODPC if processing personal data
- **ODPC registration is likely required** before NantoHealth launches

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Data Protection Act 2019"
**ODPC website:** https://www.odpc.go.ke/

---

### Kenya Information and Communications Act (Cap. 411A) and Amendments
Governs electronic communications, electronic signatures, and digital contracts.

**Key coverage:**
- Electronic signatures are legally valid (equivalent to wet signatures)
- Electronic contracts are enforceable
- Electronic records are admissible as evidence

**Nanto relevance:** Zoho Sign electronic signatures are valid in Kenya under this Act. NDA and Mudharabah agreements signed via Zoho Sign are legally enforceable.

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Kenya Information and Communications Act"

## 5. EMPLOYMENT & LABOUR

### Employment Act 2007 (No. 11 of 2007)
The primary employment statute in Kenya.

**Key coverage:**
- Employment contracts: minimum terms, written contracts
- Working hours, leave (annual, sick, maternity, paternity)
- Termination: notice periods, redundancy, summary dismissal
- Disciplinary procedures
- Non-compete clauses in employment: enforceable only if reasonable
- Discrimination and harassment protections

**Nanto relevance:** Every person Nanto pays for work — employees or contractors. Key provisions:
- Written contract required for all employees
- Notice period: minimum 28 days (or payment in lieu)
- Annual leave: minimum 21 working days per year
- Maternity leave: 3 months on full pay

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Employment Act 2007"

## 6. TAX & FISCAL

### Income Tax Act (Cap. 470)
Governs corporate income tax, PAYE (pay-as-you-earn), and withholding tax.

**Key coverage:**
- Corporate tax rate: 30% for resident companies (25% for newly listed companies)
- PAYE: employer obligation to deduct and remit employees' income tax
- Withholding tax: on dividends, interest, royalties, service fees paid to non-residents
- Transfer pricing: apply arm's length principle for related-party transactions

**Nanto relevance:**
- Nanto Ventures Limited is liable for corporate income tax
- Mudharabah distributions: verify tax treatment with KRA — profit share to investors may be subject to withholding tax
- KRA PIN is required for all formal business activity (Nanto PIN: P052406460F)

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Income Tax Act Cap 470"

---

### Value Added Tax Act 2013 (No. 35 of 2013)
Governs VAT in Kenya. Standard rate: 16%.

**Key provision for Nanto:**
The Second Schedule (Zero-rated) and Third Schedule (Exempt) list medical supplies.
- Verify whether dialysis spare parts, dialyzers, and bloodlines are zero-rated, exempt, or standard-rated
- **This is critical** — VAT exemption significantly affects pricing competitiveness
- Fetch the current schedule from kenyalaw.org and KRA

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Value Added Tax Act 2013"
**KRA tariff lookup:** https://www.kra.go.ke/

### East African Community Customs Management Act 2004 (EAC CMA)
Governs customs duties across the EAC common external tariff.

**Nanto relevance:**

## 7. COMPETITION & DISTRIBUTION

### Competition Act 2010 (No. 12 of 2010)
Governs competition, market dominance, mergers, and restrictive trade practices.

**Key coverage:**
- Restrictive trade practices: price-fixing, market-sharing, bid-rigging are prohibited
- Exclusive dealing agreements: permitted if they do not substantially lessen competition
- Abuse of dominant position: prohibited
- Merger notification: required above certain thresholds

**Nanto relevance:**
- HEDclip exclusive Africa distribution: permitted under Kenya law as long as Nanto does not hold a dominant position (unlikely at current scale)
- Any future exclusive distribution arrangement with a supplier should be documented and reviewed against this Act
- Competition Authority of Kenya (CAK) is the regulator

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Competition Act 2010"

## 8. ANTI-CORRUPTION & ETHICS

### Anti-Corruption and Economic Crimes Act 2003 (No. 3 of 2003) (ACECA)
Criminalises bribery, corruption, and economic crimes in Kenya.

**Key coverage:**
- Corruption: giving, receiving, or soliciting of advantage to influence a public official
- Facilitation payments: arguably prohibited (Kenya is a signatory to UNCAC)
- Penalties: imprisonment up to 10 years, fines, asset recovery

**Nanto relevance — critical:**
- Zero tolerance for facilitation payments is both a Nanto company principle and a legal obligation
- PPB, SHA, hospital procurement: any payment to officials to expedite approvals is illegal
- Document all interactions with public officials

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Anti-Corruption and Economic Crimes Act 2003"

---

## 10. INVESTMENT & FUNDRAISING

### Capital Markets Act (Cap. 485A)
Governs public fundraising, securities, and the capital markets.

**Key provision for Nanto:**
A private Mudharabah offer to a small number of known investors (fewer than 50 persons, not offered to the public) is a private placement and does NOT require Capital Markets Authority (CMA) approval under the Act. If Nanto were to advertise the investment publicly or offer to more than 50 persons, CMA licensing requirements would apply.

**Nanto relevance:** Current private Mudharabah raise (targeting 5–10 investors) is not a public offering. No CMA approval required. Maintain records showing it was a private, invited raise.

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Capital Markets Act Cap 485A"

---

### Proceeds of Crime and Anti-Money Laundering Act 2009 (POCAMLA) (No. 9 of 2009)
Requires due diligence on customers and investors to prevent money laundering.

**Nanto relevance:**
- For each investor, conduct basic KYC: verify identity, confirm source of funds is legitimate
- Keep records: copy of national ID/passport, confirmation of bank account origin
- Not formally regulated for anti-money laundering (no POCAMLA licence required) but basic investor KYC is good practice and may be required by DIB Bank

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Proceeds of Crime and Anti-Money Laundering Act 2009"

## 11. ISLAMIC FINANCE (NON-KENYA STATUTE)

Kenya has no standalone Islamic finance legislation. Islamic finance products are governed by:

1. **Law of Contract Act (Cap. 23):** Mudharabah is a valid commercial contract under Kenya contract law.
2. **Companies Act 2015:** Profit-sharing arrangements between parties are permitted.
3. **CBK Guidelines on Islamic Banking (2014 and updates):** The Central Bank of Kenya has issued guidelines for Islamic banking products offered by licensed banks. While Nanto is not a bank, these guidelines reflect regulatory acceptance of the structures.
4. **AAOIFI Standards:** The Accounting and Auditing Organisation for Islamic Financial Institutions publishes international standards for Mudharabah (FAS 4, SS 13). Kenya courts have not directly applied AAOIFI, but these standards inform the scholar review.

**Research path for Islamic finance questions:**
```
WebFetch: https://www.centralbank.go.ke/ — search "Islamic banking guidelines"
WebSearch: "AAOIFI Mudharabah standard FAS 4 2020"
WebSearch: "Kenya High Court Islamic finance contract case law"
```
