## KEY DISTINCTION

| Classification | What Nanto charges on output | Input VAT recoverable? | Net effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard-rated (16%) | 16% VAT added to price | YES | Nanto is a pass-through; VAT-registered customers can recover. Non-registered customers absorb the cost. |
| Zero-rated (0%) | No VAT charged | YES | Best outcome — no VAT cost to customer, but Nanto recovers all input VAT on costs |
| Exempt | No VAT charged | NO | VAT on imports and costs becomes an irrecoverable cost embedded in price. VAT registration provides no benefit. |

## WHAT THE VAT ACT SAYS ABOUT MEDICAL SUPPLIES

### Second Schedule (Zero-rated) — Medical Items
The VAT Act 2013 Second Schedule zero-rates certain medical supplies. The scope
of what is zero-rated vs. exempt vs. standard-rated in Kenya has changed several
times through Finance Acts. As at the 2023/2024 period [TRAINING]:

**Likely zero-rated (Second Schedule) — Kenya:**
- Medicaments and pharmaceutical preparations
- Some medical equipment and apparatus (the list is specific — verify)
- Goods used exclusively in the provision of medical services

**Likely exempt (Third Schedule) — Kenya:**
- Some medical services (health services provided by licensed facilities)
- Certain basic necessities

**Likely standard-rated (16%):**
- Medical equipment not specifically listed in the Schedules
- Spare parts for equipment (not the same as the equipment itself)
- Accessories and ancillary items

**THE PROBLEM:** Kenya's VAT schedules for medical devices are narrowly drafted.
There is a real risk that dialysis spare parts (gear pumps, transducers, sensors)
are treated as standard-rated (16%) industrial components, not as zero-rated
medical devices. This is a live, contested question.

## PRODUCT-BY-PRODUCT ANALYSIS

### 1. Dialysis Machine Spare Parts
(Gear pumps, pressure transducers, flow sensors, valve components, electronic
control boards, LCD replacements, connectors)

**Nature:** Component parts for medical equipment (hemodialysis machines).
Not a complete device — standalone components.

**VAT risk assessment:** HIGH UNCERTAINTY

Arguments for zero-rating or exemption:
- Used exclusively in dialysis machines (medical use only)
- HS classification under 9018 (medical instruments) may carry into VAT treatment
- Some Finance Acts have specifically zero-rated medical devices — verify current

Arguments for standard-rating:
- Spare parts are not the same as a complete medical device
- KRA may classify a gear pump as an industrial pump (Chapter 84) not a medical instrument
- The VAT schedules are specific — if spare parts are not explicitly listed, default is 16%

**Action required:**
1. Obtain HS code confirmation from clearing agent for each SKU
2. Submit formal written query to KRA requesting VAT classification ruling for each
   product category (spare parts for hemodialysis machines, HS 9018.90.90)
3. Obtain written CPA opinion from ICPAK-registered practitioner

**Until classification is resolved:** Price conservatively (assume 16% VAT may
apply). Do not advertise zero-VAT pricing until confirmed in writing.

## HOW TO GET A KRA VAT RULING

A formal KRA ruling (called a "Private Ruling" or "Tax Opinion") gives Nanto
certainty on the VAT classification of its products. The ruling is binding on KRA
for the period it covers.

**Process:**
1. Write to KRA Commissioner for Domestic Taxes (via formal letter on company letterhead)
2. State: company name, KRA PIN (P052406460F), the specific product(s), the HS code,
   the intended use, and ask for confirmation of the applicable VAT rate
3. Attach: product technical specification, HS code classification from clearing agent,
   supplier documentation showing the product's medical nature
4. KRA responds within 30–60 days (target — in practice may take longer)
5. Keep the ruling on file — it is your defence in the event of a KRA audit

**Alternative:** Engage an ICPAK-registered CPA with customs/VAT specialisation
to give a written opinion. Less authoritative than a KRA ruling but faster.

**KRA address for tax queries:** Commissioner for Domestic Taxes, KRA, Times Tower,
Haile Selassie Avenue, Nairobi. Also submit via itax.kra.go.ke (correspondence section).

## IMPORT VAT — PRACTICAL IMPACT

Whether dialysis goods are zero-rated, exempt, or standard-rated at the point of
import affects cash flow directly:

| Scenario | Impact on each KES 100,000 import |
|---|---|
| Standard-rated (16%) + Nanto VAT registered | Pay KES 16,000 at import. Recover as input VAT on next VAT return. Cash flow timing issue but no permanent cost. |
| Standard-rated (16%) + Nanto NOT registered | Pay KES 16,000 at import. This is a permanent cost embedded in COGS. Raises break-even price. |
| Zero-rated (0%) | Pay KES 0 import VAT. No cash flow issue. |
| Exempt | Pay KES 0 import VAT. But also cannot recover input VAT on any costs. Same net as zero-rated for imports, but different for Kenya-side costs. |

**Nanto's current position:** Not VAT registered. All import VAT (if standard-rated)
is a permanent cost until registered. This is why resolving the VAT classification
question is URGENT.
