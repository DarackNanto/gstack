## 1. CORPORATE INCOME TAX

### Income Tax Act (Cap. 470)
The primary statute governing corporate income tax, PAYE, withholding tax,
and instalment tax.

**Key coverage:**
- Section 3: Basis of taxation (resident company — worldwide income)
- Section 7 / First Schedule: Tax rates (30% resident companies)
- Section 12: Instalment tax — 4 payments, 20th of 4th, 6th, 9th, 12th months
- Section 15: Allowable deductions (the "deductible expenses" section)
- Section 16: Non-allowable deductions
- Second Schedule: Capital allowances (investment deduction, wear and tear)
  - Paragraph 1: Wear and tear allowances
  - Paragraph 24: Investment deduction (100% for qualifying assets)
- Section 35: Withholding tax obligations and rates
- Part III: Pay As You Earn (PAYE)

**Nanto relevance:**
- 30% CIT on net taxable income
- Pre-commencement expense deductibility in year one of trading
- Capital allowances on equipment purchases
- Withholding tax on consultant fees, director fees, dividends
- Mudharabah distribution tax treatment — unresolved, KRA ruling required

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Income Tax Act Cap 470"
**Finance Act check:** Verify current rates (30% may change), instalment tax
calculation method, and investment deduction qualifying categories each year.

## 2. VALUE ADDED TAX

### Value Added Tax Act 2013 (No. 35 of 2013)
Governs all VAT in Kenya. Standard rate 16%.

**Key coverage:**
- Section 5: What constitutes a taxable supply
- Section 6: Standard rate (16%)
- Section 14: Registration obligation (KES 5M threshold) and voluntary registration
- Section 17: Tax invoice requirements (mandatory fields)
- Section 20: Monthly return filing (20th of following month)
- Section 29: Refunds of excess input tax
- Second Schedule: Zero-rated supplies (0% — input VAT recoverable)
- Third Schedule: Exempt supplies (no VAT charged — input VAT NOT recoverable)

**Nanto relevance — CRITICAL:**
- VAT classification of dialysis spare parts, dialyzers, and bloodlines determines
  pricing structure, margin, and competitive positioning
- See VAT-MEDICAL-DEVICES.md for the detailed analysis
- Registration timing decision: voluntary registration may be advantageous if
  significant input VAT is incurred before the KES 5M turnover threshold is hit

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Value Added Tax Act 2013"
**KRA VAT guidance:** https://www.kra.go.ke/individual/filing-paying/types-of-taxes/vat
**Finance Act check:** Second and Third Schedules amended regularly — verify current
classification of medical devices each year.

## 3. TAX ADMINISTRATION

### Tax Procedures Act 2015 (No. 29 of 2015)
Governs how KRA administers all taxes — record keeping, assessments, objections,
penalties, voluntary disclosure, and the audit process.

**Key coverage:**
- Section 23: Obligation to keep records (5 years minimum)
- Section 25: KRA's power to access records and premises
- Section 31: Self-assessment — taxpayer files and pays; KRA may audit later
- Section 33: KRA may issue an amended assessment within 5 years of filing
- Section 51: Objection to a KRA assessment (30 days to object)
- Section 56: Appeal to Tax Appeals Tribunal (TAT)
- Section 83: Voluntary disclosure — reduced penalties if taxpayer self-reports
- Section 84: Offences and penalties (including criminal liability for tax evasion)
- Late filing penalties: KES 10,000 or 5% of tax due, whichever is higher

**Nanto relevance:**
- 5-year record retention is the absolute minimum — keep all records
- Understand the 30-day window to object to any KRA assessment
- Voluntary disclosure before KRA identifies an issue reduces penalties
- Do not communicate directly with KRA during an audit — engage a CPA

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Tax Procedures Act 2015"

## 4. CUSTOMS AND IMPORT DUTY

### East African Community Customs Management Act 2004 (EAC CMA)
Governs all imports into Kenya under the EAC common external tariff framework.

**Key coverage:**
- EAC Common External Tariff (CET): 0% raw materials, 10% intermediate, 25% finished goods
- Sensitive goods list: certain products (including many medical devices) attract 0%
- Import Declaration Fee (IDF): 3.5% of CIF value
- Railway Development Levy (RDL): 2% of CIF value
- HS (Harmonised System) code classification determines duty rate
- Misclassification is a customs offence — retrospective assessment plus penalties

**Nanto relevance:**
- Imports from China (CN Meditech), India (Shubham), Denmark (HEDclip)
- Dialysis machines and most HS 9018.90.90 items likely 0% under CET
- Total import cost = CIF + Duty + IDF (3.5%) + RDL (2%) + VAT on landed value
- Always use a licensed clearing agent at JKIA — Box Leo (3PL) assists with this

**EAC search:** https://www.eac.int/customs/
**KenTrade (IDF filing):** https://www.kentrade.go.ke/
**Finance Act check:** EAC CET schedules updated periodically; Kenya also imposes
additional levies via Finance Act — verify current IDF and RDL rates each year.

## 5. EXCISE DUTY

### Excise Duty Act 2015 (No. 23 of 2015)
Governs excise duty on specific goods (alcohol, tobacco, fuel, financial services,
certain imports).

**Nanto relevance:** LOW — dialysis equipment and medical consumables are generally
not excisable goods. However, verify that none of Nanto's products (HEDclips,
connectors, electronic boards) are classified as excisable goods under the Act's
schedules. Confirm with clearing agent.

**kenyalaw.org search:** "Excise Duty Act 2015"

## 6. DOUBLE TAXATION AGREEMENTS (DTAs)

Kenya has signed DTAs with several countries relevant to Nanto's supply chain:

| Country | DTA status | WHT rate on dividends | WHT rate on services |
|---|---|---|---|
| India | DTA in force | Reduced rate | Reduced rate |
| Denmark | DTA in force | Reduced rate | Reduced rate |
| China | DTA in force | Reduced rate | Reduced rate |

**Nanto relevance:** When Nanto pays service fees (not goods purchases) to suppliers
in India, Denmark or China, the DTA may reduce the Kenya withholding tax rate below
the domestic rate. For goods purchases, WHT generally does not apply — DTAs become
relevant for technical service fees, royalties, or management fees.

**Verify current DTA rates:** https://www.kra.go.ke/ — search "Double Taxation Agreements"
**kenyalaw.org search:** "Double Taxation Agreement India / China / Denmark"

## 7. TRANSFER PRICING

### Income Tax Act Cap 470 — Transfer Pricing Rules (Section 18 and subsidiary rules)
**+ Income Tax (Transfer Pricing) Rules 2006**

Transfer pricing rules apply when Nanto transacts with related parties (companies
under common ownership or control).

**Nanto relevance:** Currently LOW — Nanto Ventures Limited buys from unrelated
third-party suppliers (CN Meditech, Shubham, HEDclip). Transfer pricing rules
would apply if Nanto later forms a related entity (e.g., a holding company, or
buys from a company Darack also controls). Document all transactions at arm's length.
