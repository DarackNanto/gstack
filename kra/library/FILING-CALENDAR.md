# KRA Filing Calendar — Nanto Ventures Limited
## All KRA deadlines for a December year-end Kenya company
### Last reviewed: 11 May 2026

> **[TRAINING — verify]** Dates and rates in this calendar are based on the
> Income Tax Act Cap 470, VAT Act 2013, and Tax Procedures Act 2015 as at
> the knowledge cutoff date. The Finance Act (passed annually, typically
> June/July) can change deadlines, rates, and thresholds. Verify the current
> year's Finance Act before each filing season.
>
> Late filing carries automatic penalties under Tax Procedures Act 2015:
> KES 10,000 or 5% of tax due, whichever is higher. Plus interest on
> outstanding tax at 1% per month (verify current rate).

## MONTHLY OBLIGATIONS

### VAT Return (when registered)
- **Frequency:** Every month, including months with zero activity
- **Due date:** 20th day of the month following the tax period
- **Platform:** iTax → Returns → VAT → VAT 3 return
- **What to file:** Total output VAT, total input VAT, net payable or refund claim
- **Pay by:** Same date as filing (20th of following month)
- **Nil return:** Must still be filed even if no transactions in the month

| Month of transactions | Filing deadline |
|---|---|
| January | 20 February |
| February | 20 March |
| March | 20 April |
| April | 20 May |
| May | 20 June |
| June | 20 July |
| July | 20 August |
| August | 20 September |
| September | 20 October |
| October | 20 November |
| November | 20 December |
| December | 20 January |

---

### PAYE (when Nanto has employees)
- **Frequency:** Monthly
- **Due date:** 9th of the month following payroll
- **Platform:** iTax → PAYE → P10 monthly return
- **Currently:** NOT ACTIVE — no employees

---

### Withholding Tax (WHT)
- **Frequency:** Monthly (when applicable — i.e., when WHT-triggering payments are made)
- **Due date:** 20th of the month following the deduction
- **Platform:** iTax → Withholding Tax return
- **Applies when:** Nanto pays director's fees, consultant/professional fees, rent,
  royalties, or (if applicable) investor distributions

## QUARTERLY OBLIGATIONS

### Instalment Tax (Corporate Income Tax advance payments)
**Basis:** Estimated annual CIT liability paid in four equal instalments.
**Calculation:** Use prior year tax as the base, or a reasonable estimate of the
current year liability. Underpayment attracts interest.

| Instalment | Percentage | Due date |
|---|---|---|
| 1st instalment | 25% of estimated annual CIT | **20 April** |
| 2nd instalment | 25% of estimated annual CIT | **20 June** |
| 3rd instalment | 25% of estimated annual CIT | **20 September** |
| 4th instalment | 25% of estimated annual CIT | **20 December** |

**If Nanto has no taxable income in the year:** File nil instalment returns.
Do not skip filing — a missed return is a penalty even if no tax is due.

**Platform:** iTax → Income Tax → Company → IT2C (Instalment Tax)

## ANNUAL OBLIGATIONS

### Corporate Income Tax (CIT) Annual Return
- **Return type:** IT2 (Company annual return)
- **Due date:** **30 June** (6 months after December year end)
- **Pay by:** Same date — any balance of tax after instalments
- **Attach:** Annual accounts (Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet), tax computation
- **Platform:** iTax → Income Tax → Company → IT2

**Year-end checklist before filing:**
- [ ] Zoho Books accounts finalised and reconciled
- [ ] All income recognised and documented
- [ ] All deductions supported by invoices/receipts on file
- [ ] Capital allowances schedule prepared (equipment, computers)
- [ ] Pre-commencement expenses schedule (first year only)
- [ ] Bad debts documented (if any provisioned)
- [ ] WHT certificates received for any income subject to WHT
- [ ] Transfer pricing documentation (if transacting with related parties)
- [ ] CPA review before submission

### VAT Annual Reconciliation
Not a separate return in Kenya, but the December VAT return (due 20 January) closes
the VAT year. Ensure the December return tallies with the annual accounts.

### P9 Certificates to Employees (when applicable)
- **Due date:** 28 February (for the prior calendar year)
- **What:** Tax deduction certificate issued to each employee showing PAYE deducted
- **Currently:** NOT ACTIVE — no employees

## PENALTIES SUMMARY

| Offence | Penalty (TRAINING — verify current rates) |
|---|---|
| Late VAT return | KES 10,000 or 5% of tax due, whichever is higher |
| Late CIT return | KES 10,000 or 5% of tax due, whichever is higher |
| Late PAYE remittance | 25% of tax due + interest |
| Late WHT remittance | 25% of tax due + interest |
| Outstanding tax interest | 1% per month on outstanding balance |
| Tax evasion (criminal) | Up to 200% of tax due + imprisonment |

**Important:** A nil return filed late still attracts the KES 10,000 flat penalty.
Always file on time, even if the return is nil.

## RECORD RETENTION SCHEDULE

Under Tax Procedures Act 2015, Section 23:

| Record type | Minimum retention |
|---|---|
| All accounting records (Zoho Books exports) | 5 years |
| Import documentation (IDF, customs entries, duty receipts) | 5 years |
| Sales and purchase invoices | 5 years |
| Bank statements | 5 years |
| Employee and payroll records | 5 years |
| Contracts and agreements (investor, supplier, customer) | 5 years from expiry |
| KRA filings and payment receipts | 5 years |
| WHT certificates issued/received | 5 years |

**Practical guidance:** Export Zoho Books annually (full company backup to PDF and
CSV). Store on WorkDrive with folder: `30-FINANCE/Tax-Records/[Year]/`.
Keep original import documents (customs entries) as PDFs linked to the purchase
invoice in Zoho Books.
